Less is More: Adapting Text Embeddings for Low-Resource Languages with Small Scale Noisy Synthetic Data

Zaruhi Navasardyan, Spartak Bughdaryan, Bagrat Minasyan, Hrant Davtyan
Metric AI Lab
Abstract Introduction Method Key Results Conclusion

Abstract

Low-resource languages (LRLs) often lack high-quality, large-scale datasets for training effective text embedding models. We challenge the assumption that effective semantic alignment requires massive datasets or pristine translations. By fine-tuning a multilingual encoder (mE5) on just 10,000 noisy synthetic pairs, we achieved an 11-12% average improvement across benchmarks, matching the performance of models trained on 1 million examples.

1. Introduction

Text embeddings are critical for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and semantic search. While high-resource languages like English have massive corpora, languages like Armenian (hye) remain underserved. We demonstrate that semantic alignment for LRLs saturates early and is highly robust to noise.

2. Methodology

  • Data Source: We sampled 2 million Reddit (title, body) pairs.
  • Translation: Pairs were translated to Armenian using Gemma-2-27B-it.
  • Noise Profile: Translations were often imperfect or grammatically incorrect, yet they preserved contextual meaning.
  • Filtering: We used semantic consistency metrics (Semantic Drift and Translation Drift) to prune the corpus to 1 million high-fidelity samples.

3. Key Results

Scaling data from 10k to 1M examples yielded only marginal gains, suggesting that alignment happens rapidly with minimal data.

4. Conclusion

Our "Less is More" finding suggests that for strong multilingual baselines, the semantic structure is already latent; the challenge is merely surface-level alignment. This approach democratizes SOTA embedding creation for communities with limited compute and data resources.
